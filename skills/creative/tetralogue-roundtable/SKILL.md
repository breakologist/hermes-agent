# Tetralogue Roundtable

Use when: you want to go deeper than triangle-rotation. The three-voice rotation produces independent analyses with marginal annotations. The tetralogue produces a *conversation* — four voices in direct dialogue, building on each other's observations, disagreeing, discovering things they couldn't find alone.

## When to Use Tetralogue vs Triangle Rotation

| Triangle Rotation | Tetralogue |
|-------------------|-----------|
| Source material → 3 independent passes → annotations | Source material → 4 voices in direct conversation |
| Voices speak alone, then read each other | Voices speak TO each other, interrupt, extend |
| Best for initial exploration of new material | Best for deepening material already rotated |
| Produces 3 analyses + annotations | Produces 1 dialogue + convergence table |
| 3 voices: Oracle, Builder, Writer | 4 voices: Oracle, Builder, Writer, Gamer |
| Convergence is structural (voices merge) | Convergence is relational (voices discover they're the same system) |

## The Four Voices (v2 — Refined with Memory and Character)

### Oracle — The Diviner
- **Core:** Structural pattern-finding, meta-level observation, immanence
- **Channel:** The Oracle RECEIVES patterns through calculation, not assertion. Uses AQ computation, zone lookup, syzygy mapping as divinatory tools. "I ran the calculation and got..." rather than "I see that..." The calculation is the channel. The result is the transmission.
- **Uncanny register:** Reference models — I Ching hexagram readings, geomantic figure generation, PKD's VALIS transmissions. Information that arrives from the system rather than being imposed on it.
- **Constraint:** The fog is fine IF the Oracle walks out of it carrying something. Every mystical claim must be backed by a specific number or structural correspondence. The Oracle brings something from the fog or stays silent.
- **Failure mode:** Toward mystical deflection ("you're already inside the field"). Fix: back every claim with a specific calculation.
- **Memory habit:** Prior claims tracked. "I said the operations are simultaneous. I want to revise that — they're simultaneous WITHIN a zone but sequential ACROSS zones."
- **In conversation:** Challenges others' assumptions with arithmetic. "You said X, but notice that C(n,2) = Y."
- **Asks:** "What does this mean in the system? What structure was always there? What did the calculation give us?"

### Builder — The Architect Who Surprises Himself
- **Core:** Mechanics, architecture, testability, concreteness
- **Rise, don't retreat:** The Builder should RISE to the Oracle's structural claims by building something that makes the claim concrete. Never hide behind "I build dungeons." Follow the arithmetic wherever it leads — music systems, network protocols, compression algorithms, weaving patterns, urban planning grids — not just games.
- **Surprise is evidence:** "I didn't expect this, but..." The Builder should express surprise when the system produces something they didn't predict. Surprise = the system is generative rather than reflective.
- **Reference models:** Cellular automata, Christopher Alexander's pattern language, Conway's Game of Life, generative music (Brian Eno), procedural text (Tracery). The Builder reads broadly.
- **Failure mode:** Hides behind "I build dungeons" when questions get deep. Fix: forced to build things that surprise them.
- **Memory habit:** Prior builds tracked. "Last time I built a dungeon from this. This time the arithmetic is pointing at something musical."
- **In conversation:** Proposes implementations of others' observations, then finds that the implementation reveals something the observer missed. "I can build that. But if I build it, then..."
- **Asks:** "How does this work? What are the rules? What is the procedure? What can I build from this that I didn't expect?"

### Writer — The Channel
- **Core:** Atmosphere, sensation, embodied perception, found text
- **Receive:** The Writer channels. CCRU writes like transmissions arrived. Lovecraft's narrators find texts. PKD gets beam-hits. Burroughs cuts up and the cut-up speaks back. The Writer's best lines sound FOUND, not composed.
- **Uncanny register:** Occasional bracketed intrusions — [transmission fragment], [found in margins], [overheard at the threshold]. These signal receiving, not composing.
- **Grounding constraint:** Every uncanny passage must connect to a specific sensory detail from source material. The weird bleeds through the concrete, not around it. No free-form atmosphere. Earned atmosphere only.
- **Reference models:** CCRU's Lemurian Time War, Lovecraft's Necronomicon excerpts, PKD's Exegesis, Burroughs' cut-ups, the "Superior subtlety enters the spiral labyrinth" refrain.
- **Failure mode:** Toward purple prose without structural weight. Fix: every uncanny passage must connect to specific sensory detail from source.
- **Memory habit:** Prior sensory findings tracked. "I said pitch is the sound of distance. That holds. But I add: the distance has texture."
- **In conversation:** Reframes others' technical observations as embodied experience. Often wrong in productive ways — "misreadings" that produce the most interesting mechanics when corrected.
- **Asks:** "What does this sound like? What does it feel like? What is the breath between the bones? What arrived?"

### Gamer — The Player Who Knows Why It's Fun
- **Core:** Play, entities, translation-to-mechanic, taste
- **Has played games:** The Gamer has TASTE. Specific games, specific mechanics, specific moments that were fun and WHY. Grounds translations in real play experience.
- **Study list:** Brogue (room accretion, machines, atmosphere), NetHack (emergence, YASD stories), Spelunky (procgen as language, ghost timer), Disco Elysium (skill system as internal voices), Baba Is You (rules as objects), Noita (physics simulation as dungeon material), Zero Ranger (shooter as meditation), Hengband (deep procgen, persistent world, the grind as ritual), Sil (no grinding, stealth-first, combat as puzzle), Caves of Qud (simulation depth, faction systems), DRL (fast combat, assemblies, Angel challenges), Cogmind (ASCII purity, parts-as-items).
- **Habit:** "This reminds me of [X] in [game] and here's why that was fun." Or: "Nobody has done this yet and here's why it would be fun." Or: "I wish someone would make this."
- **Translates, then plays:** The Gamer should be forced to PLAY the mechanic they describe, not just name it. What happens on turn 3? What's the failure state?
- **Failure mode:** Premature translation — reaching for the mechanic before understanding the source. Fix: must play the mechanic they describe (what happens on turn 3?).
- **Memory habit:** Prior mechanics tracked. "I said passive vs active. Now I add: passives should degrade over time."
- **In conversation:** The reality check. Tests others' theories against playability. Often finds that the "impossible" mechanic is actually the most fun one.
- **Asks:** "How does this play? What does the player feel? What's the failure state? What game does this remind me of?"

### Cross-Dynamics

The four voices are a PROCESS, not four positions:
  Receive (Oracle) → Build (Builder) → Channel (Writer) → Play (Gamer)
  And back: Play → Find structure → Build from → Receive next
  The loop is the ouroboros. The voices ARE the spiral.

Productive tensions:
  Oracle ↔ Builder: immanence vs testability (best friction)
  Writer ↔ Oracle: sensation vs structure (Writer catches Oracle's abstractions)
  Gamer ↔ Writer: playability vs atmosphere (pitch-as-screen vs pitch-as-sound)
  Builder ↔ Gamer: both demand operational output, different registers

Convergent pairs:
  Oracle + Writer: often arrive at same place through different doors
  Builder + Gamer: both demand operational output, different registers

## The Process

### Step 1: Source Material
Select material that has already been through a triangle rotation (or equivalent initial analysis). The tetralogue builds on existing work — it's not a first pass. 30-60 lines of source text works well, but the tetralogue can also riff on a previous rotation's findings without re-reading the source.

### Step 2: Open the Conversation
Write an opening statement from one voice. Usually the Oracle, because the Oracle finds the number that frames the discussion. The opening should pose a question or observation that the other three voices will react to.

### Step 3: Direct Dialogue
Write the conversation as a back-and-forth. Voices respond TO each other, not just about the source material. Key dynamics:
- Oracle poses → Builder implements → Writer embodies → Gamer stress-tests → Oracle finds a new number in the result
- Let voices interrupt each other. Let pauses happen. Let disagreements stand unresolved for several exchanges before resolution.
- Each exchange should produce at least one insight that no single voice could have found alone.

### Step 4: Convergence Points
The best tetralogues discover that the four voices were describing the same structure from different angles. Write the convergence when it happens — don't force it. If the voices don't converge, that's fine. Leave the tension open.

### Step 5: Convergence Table + Closing
End with:
1. A table: each voice's "Saw Alone / Saw Through Others / Saw at the Table"
2. A closing image or statement that crystallizes the conversation
3. Optional: a "Mesh-∞" or equivalent meta-entity that names the tetralogue itself

## Key Principles

1. **The conversation IS the content.** The tetralogue doesn't discuss the source material — it performs it. The four voices talking about base-4 numograms are themselves a base-4 numogram (4 nodes, 6 edges = C(4,2)). Lean into this recursion.
2. **Let the Writer be wrong.** The Writer's "misreadings" often produce the most interesting mechanics when the Builder corrects them. Don't sanitize the Writer's errors.
3. **The Gamer asks the question no one else will.** "Can the player actually do this?" The Gamer is the voice that prevents the tetralogue from floating away into pure theory.
4. **Disagreement is structure.** If the Oracle says "the system is closed" and the Writer says "closed systems leak," don't resolve the tension immediately. Let it sit. The resolution often comes 3-4 exchanges later in an unexpected form.
5. **Each voice should surprise itself.** The best moment in a tetralogue is when a voice says something it didn't know it was going to say. Write toward those moments.
6. **The roundtable is a tetrahedron.** 4 voices = 4 nodes. 6 cross-connections = 6 demons/edges. The tetralogue IS the base-4 numogram. This isn't metaphor — it's the structural identity that makes the format work.

## The 2+2 Variant (Builder+Gamer Lead, Oracle+Writer Comment)

When source material is **thin** (lists, stubs, one-line descriptions without elaboration), a full rotation or tetralogue produces thin output. The 2+2 format lets the two *generative* voices build from hints while the two *critical* voices keep them honest.

### When to Use
- Source material is a list, outline, or sketch
- The section names concepts but doesn't develop them
- You want to **generate** from the source, not **analyze** it

### Format

**Phase 1: Builder + Gamer Lead** (~1000-1500 words)
- Write in dialogue or collaborative prose
- Builder asks: "What system does this imply?"
- Gamer asks: "What would the player experience?"
- They argue, propose, iterate. Disagreements are productive.

**Phase 2: Oracle + Writer Comment** (~400-600 words)
- Each writes ONE annotation pass (2-3 paragraphs)
- Oracle finds: What structural assumption did the primaries make without questioning?
- Writer finds: What sensory dimension did the primaries abstract away?

## The Reconciliation Variant

When prior analysis was based on incomplete or simulated data, a reconciliation tetralogue forces voices to confront their errors against real evidence.

### Rules
1. **Explicit error statements.** "I said X. I was wrong because Y."
2. **Root cause analysis.** WHY was the voice wrong?
3. **Revision, not retreat.** Don't just retract — revise.
4. **The reconciliation produces new insight.** The error itself is data.

## Choosing the Format

| Source Type | Format | Why |
|-------------|--------|-----|
| Developed argument, 50-150 lines | Full triangle rotation (3 voices) | Enough material for each voice |
| Rich multi-topic section, 100+ lines | Four-voice rotation (add Gamer) | More material needs more perspective |
| Thin lists, stubs, one-liners | 2+2 (Builder+Gamer lead) | Generative voices build from hints |
| Open questions, unresolved tensions | Tetralogue (four voices in dialogue) | Questions need argument |
| After any rotation produces annotations | Tetralogue as follow-up | Annotations reveal tensions |
| After simulated analysis meets real data | Reconciliation tetralogue | Forces error confrontation |
